Section 2 — Company Narrative and Executive Summary

There is a difference between a vendor who makes videos and a partner who shapes how an institution is perceived

The committee reviewing this proposal carries a specific responsibility. Over the next two years, the vendor selected here will shape how tens of thousands of prospective students first encounter Vermont State University — across six campuses, dozens of programs, and every digital channel where enrollment decisions are made. That is not a typical production contract. It is a brand stewardship responsibility. Zarrin Productions is submitting this proposal because the combination of academic background, production capacity, and direct understanding of how higher education communicates makes this precisely the kind of engagement we were built for — and because VTSU's story, at this particular moment in the institution's history, is genuinely worth telling well.

Zarrin Productions is a full-service video production and digital marketing agency with over 1,000 videos produced across education, healthcare, technology, and brand storytelling. More than 100 in-house professionals operate as one cohesive production operation — creative directors, editors, motion designers, strategists, and digital marketers working together under one strategic roof.

Universities today operate in a most competitive digital enrollment landscape in a generation. Prospective students encounter dozens of institutional messages before making a decision. Programs that cannot communicate their value visually and compellingly lose ground to those that can — regardless of academic quality. Strategic video storytelling is no longer a marketing enhancement. For institutions like VTSU, it is a direct enrollment tool. That is the environment this engagement is designed to address, and it is the environment Zarrin Productions was built to operate in.

The case for Zarrin begins where most agencies cannot follow. The agency's principal is a former university instructor with graduate-level credentials in both Digital Marketing and Cybersecurity. This is not a metaphor. It is a direct production advantage. The person responsible for VTSU's engagement strategy has designed instructional content for academic settings, understands how prospective students evaluate institutions, and has produced content in regulated industries where accuracy and trust are non-negotiable.

Production Workflow — Collaborative Process

— Collaborative Process

Five phases. Full transparency.
Nothing advances without approval.

Every engagement follows a documented production process. VTSU's Marketing and Communications team has a defined input point at each phase, and no stage proceeds without prior client sign-off.

1

Discovery

A structured working session with VTSU's team. Goals, audience profiles, brand guidelines, content priorities by campus and program, and performance expectations are documented and agreed upon. This session produces the brief that governs the full engagement.

2

Strategy and Concepting

Production roadmap, messaging architecture, content calendar, and per-format strategy developed by Zarrin and reviewed by VTSU. The strategy is a working document — it governs production decisions at every stage and is updated as the engagement evolves.

3

Pre-Production and Production

Scripts, shot plans, scheduling, contributor coordination, and all pre-production logistics completed and approved. Production is then executed — remote, hybrid, or via coordinated content capture — with creative direction centralized in the Zarrin team.

4

Post and Review

Editing, color, sound, motion, and finishing. VTSU reviews polished cuts at every stage — not rough assemblies. Two structured revision rounds are included per asset. Revision scope and turnaround times are agreed upon at engagement start.

5

Delivery and Performance

Final delivery in all formats with a complete delivery manifest. For retained engagements, content performance is tracked and reviewed with VTSU's team, and each production cycle is informed by data from the previous one.
